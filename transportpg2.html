Transport

How to get around Barcelona?

Barcelona has buses, trams, the metro, taxi drivers, Uber drivers and lots more. It can be hard to navigate. When you are on a budget it can make it even more difficult to decide on the best mode of transport. This guide will help you select the best transport for you.  

Barcelona Buses

Buses can be a convenient and cheap way to get around Barcelona. They also have bus services that operate at night, which can be really helpful for late night activities. So, this is one I would recommend, however be very careful pickpockets are very common on here.

Price:

single - €2.40, T-casual (valid for 10 days) - €11.35

My recommendation:

Check out Hola Barcelona they have a range of passes such as 24hr, 48hr, and 72hr allowing you to travel Barcelona. These passes also can include the metro and Barcelona hop on hop off bus tours if you select this so you can really get your money's worth.

The Metro

This operates more frequently than the buses so it could be a more convenient option for you. Barcelona has eight metro lines that can be identified by the number and colour of the line. This can be a little difficult but there are always people around to help assist you.

Price:

€2.40 single, other prices will depend on your journey.

My recommendation:

My recommendation – Ensure you plan your journey before and try to avoid travelling at peak times as the metro will become very busy making you more liable to get lost or pickpocketed.

Barcelona Trains

Although Barcelona Is a city that you can get around with trams, busses etc. you can utilise the trains to explore other parts of Spain. The train station ‘Barcelona Sants’ is a popular train station that can help you travel to places such as Madrid, Sevilla etc. The station is very large and can be confusing but there are lots of people around to help.

Price:

Depends on your journey

My recommendation:

If interrailing or simply buying a seat reservation, not a train ticket, be sure to book a slot in advance on the company's website, this will save you lots of time.

Barcelona Trams

There are 2 tram lines in Barcelona. These will help you get to shopping areas and residential zones. Moreover, the trams are accessible and fast, allowing you to have a comfortable journey. You will need to ensure you validate your ticket before you hop on the tram, however.

Price:

Single ticket: €2,40, T-casual: €11,35

My recommendation:

If you feel you will be using the trams a lot, then get yourself on Hola Barcelona pass this will provide a day pass to access on the trams saving you lots of money. You can even decide to get longer passes such as 48hrs.

Taxi / Uber / Bolt

TAll these companies operate in Barcelona, and they can be convenient but when on a budget the price can stack up. If you are with a group of friends, this is perfect to share as it could cost you a few euros. Be sure to compare prices. Sometimes taxis can be cheaper than Uber and Bolt.

Price:

Depends on your journey.

My recommendation:

If you are going to a popular landmark or a popular club such as ‘Shoko’ do not book directly to the place the price will be much higher, find a shop or street nearby to drop you off and walk from there. Please remain safe! If you prefer, they just drop you off at your destination then that is fine as well.
